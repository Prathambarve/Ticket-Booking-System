Our Journey

The Three Musketeers

What happens when 3 long-time friends go holidaying together in South Africa? The seed of a BigTree is planted. A company is planned, from roots to fruits. Soon after the Eureka moment, C.E.O. Akshay Bisht quits his job at Facebook, Co-Founder Sumit Bhakt takes over Technology, and Co-Founder Kushagra Bhatt takes over Finance.

1999

Gaining Ground With Cinemas

With the cinema industry on a high and multiplexes and large cinema chains starting to entertain Indian audiences around the country; Bigtree takes over the rights to retail and service New Zealand based ticketing software, Vista in India.

2000-01

Fighting the rough seas

In the midst of the dot com bust that happened in 2002, the company offered technology solutions from the Customer Relationship Management point of view to fight the storm. This business flourished under the leadership of our 3 musketeers.

2002-2007

The Big Bucks

Network 18 invested in March 2007. In August, the same year an internal contest was held to coin a name for the new company. A developer intern came up with the name BookMyShow.com and the rest as they say is history.

2007-2010

Launch Of Cinestar

Bigtree Entertainment Pvt. Ltd launches India's first ticketing aggregator - Cinestar- in August 2007, now one of the biggest ticketing portals in the country.

2010-2012

Need for Speed

Cinestar becomes the exclusive ticketing partner for Formula 1, the Indian Grand Prix. After the grand success of 2011, we are all geared up for 2012's extravaganza.

2012-2014

Yeh IPL Hai Boss

Cinestar becomes the official ticketing partner for Mumbai Indians, Kings XI Punjab and Delhi Daredevils. Today we have Pune Warriors and Rajasthan Royals too on board this high drama entertainment circus called the IPL.

2014

Record High!

During the opening weekend of mega blockbuster Dangal, BookMyShow creates new records; sells over 3 million movie tickets in a single weekend and for shows on Saturday & Sunday, BookMyShow sells over 1 Million tickets a day!

2015

Money Matters

SAIF Partners invests USD 25 Million i.e. Rs. 150 crore valuing BookMyShow at Rs. 1000 crores.
